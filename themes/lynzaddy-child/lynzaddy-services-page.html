Service 01

WordPress
Development

From first commit to launch day — and every rebuild, migration, and optimization in between. Every project starts with understanding your architecture before touching a template.

01
New Site Builds

Ground-up WordPress builds designed around your content model, your team's workflows, and the way your users actually move through information. Architecture first, Elementor second.

Good fit if: You're launching a new brand, spinning out a product, or starting fresh after years of patchwork.
Redesigns & Rebuilds

Take what's working, fix what isn't, and build on a foundation that won't require another rebuild in two years. We audit before we design — no demolishing things worth keeping.

Good fit if: Your site is embarrassing to share, can't be updated without a developer, or was built by someone who's no longer around.
WooCommerce Builds

E-commerce on WordPress done properly — clean checkout flows, reliable payment integrations, sane product structures, and a storefront your marketing team can manage without filing a ticket.

Good fit if: You're launching a new store, migrating from Shopify/Squarespace, or your current WooCommerce setup is held together with plugins and prayers.
Custom Theme & Child Theme Dev

Bespoke theme development for complex design systems — or a clean Elementor child theme that locks in your brand tokens and gives every future developer a sane starting point.

Good fit if: Your current theme is a pre-built you've been fighting for years, or you need a consistent design system your whole team can build on.
Performance Optimization

Core Web Vitals, page speed, image optimization, caching strategy, database cleanup, and the plugin audit that always uncovers three things nobody knew were running. Real benchmarks, before and after.

Good fit if: Your Google PageSpeed score is embarrassing, your bounce rate is high, or your host keeps telling you it's a "resource issue."
Plugin Customization

When a plugin almost does what you need, we extend it cleanly — hooks, filters, and child-plugin patterns that survive updates. No hacking core files. No edits that disappear on the next release.

Good fit if: You need a plugin to behave differently than it does out of the box, or you need two plugins to talk to each other.
Accessibility (WCAG)

WCAG 2.1 AA compliance audits and remediation — semantic HTML, keyboard navigation, ARIA, color contrast, and screen reader testing. We document everything so your team can maintain compliance after launch.

Good fit if: Your organization has legal accessibility obligations, serves a public audience, or has received an ADA inquiry.
Migrations

Host migrations, platform migrations (Squarespace, Wix, Drupal → WordPress), and CMS migrations with content mapping. Zero-downtime cutovers with a tested rollback plan — not a "fingers crossed" approach.

Good fit if: You're leaving a host that's underserving you, consolidating platforms, or finally moving off that legacy CMS IT has been asking about for three years.
"We scope it honestly. If it should take a week, we'll tell you it takes a week."

No discovery phase padding. No scope creep by design. We've been doing this long enough to know what projects actually cost — and we'd rather give you an accurate number up front than manage your expectations down the road.

Service 02

Managed
Hosting

Hosting isn't just where your site lives. It's what keeps it alive at 2am when something breaks. We offer managed environments that stay updated, monitored, backed up, and ready — without you having to think about any of it.

02
Managed WordPress Hosting

We operate the environment directly — provisioned, optimized, and monitored for WordPress specifically. No shared-hosting noise, no cPanel archaeology. You log in to a site that runs.

Good fit if: You want one throat to choke. One invoice, one call, one team who owns the full stack.
Third-Party Host Setup

Prefer WP Engine, Kinsta, Flywheel, or SiteGround? We'll recommend the right tier for your traffic and budget, set it up correctly, and hand you the keys with documentation — not a login and a wave.

Good fit if: You want to own the hosting relationship directly but need someone to pick the right environment and configure it properly.
Updates & Maintenance

Core, theme, and plugin updates tested in staging before they touch production. Not a "click update and hope" approach — a staged process that catches conflicts before your site does.

Good fit if: You have a site that's behind on updates because someone got burned once, or you simply don't have bandwidth to do it right.
Security Monitoring & Cleanup

Malware scanning, intrusion detection, firewall configuration, and emergency cleanup when something goes wrong. We've cleaned up enough hacked sites to be very motivated about prevention.

Good fit if: You've been hacked before, you're in a regulated industry, or your site is a real business asset you can't afford to have go down.
Backups & Disaster Recovery

Daily automated backups, off-site storage, and a tested restore process. Not "we have backups" — we have backups we've actually restored from. There's a difference.

Good fit if: You're not sure the last time your site was backed up, or you've never tested a restore.
Staging Environments

Every site we touch gets a staging environment. Test updates, review content changes, and approve redesigns before they're public — not in production in front of your actual users.

Good fit if: Your team currently tests changes on the live site because there's nowhere else to test them.
DNS & Domain Management

Domain transfers, DNS configuration, SSL certificates, email routing, and subdomain management. The unglamorous infrastructure work that causes the most panic when it's wrong and nobody notices when it's right.

Good fit if: You're migrating hosts, launching a new domain, or dealing with email deliverability issues tied to DNS misconfiguration.

Service 03

Consulting &
Fractional Work

Not every engagement is a build. Sometimes you need someone to look at what you have, tell you what's wrong, and help you fix it — without taking over. That's consulting. And it's where fifteen years of seeing the same mistakes pays off fast.

03
Fractional WordPress Dev

Senior WordPress expertise embedded into your team on a part-time basis. Attend standups, review PRs, answer the architecture questions your in-house team doesn't have time to research, and ship features alongside your developers.

Good fit if: You have a WordPress developer but need senior oversight, or you have a team that can implement if someone tells them what to do.
Tech Stack Audits

A formal review of your plugins, theme, hosting environment, update cadence, security posture, and architecture decisions. Delivered as a written report with prioritized recommendations — not just "this is bad."

Good fit if: You've inherited a WordPress site and want to know what you're actually dealing with before making any moves.
CMS Training & Docs

Custom documentation and live training sessions for marketing teams, content editors, and anyone who touches the CMS but didn't build it. Tailored to your specific setup — not a generic WordPress tutorial.

Good fit if: Your team avoids updating the site because they don't know what will break, or you're handing a site to a client team for the first time.
Vendor & Agency Vetting

Reviewing proposals, auditing code, interviewing candidates, and evaluating agency work product before you accept delivery. We've seen enough bad work to know what to look for — and how to describe it in writing.

Good fit if: You're about to hire a developer or agency and want a second set of senior eyes on what they're promising.
IT ↔ Marketing Liaison

We sit in the middle of the conversation that usually goes nowhere — the one between Marketing who wants to launch something and IT who needs to approve it. We translate, document, and help both teams get to yes.

Good fit if: Your marketing requests are stuck in an IT queue, or IT is getting blamed for slowing down campaigns they never agreed to support.
WordPress Retainer

A monthly block of senior development hours — yours to use for content updates, small features, bug fixes, performance work, or anything else that comes up. No ticket system. No minimum notice. Just send us the request.

Good fit if: You have a recurring list of things that never get done because nobody owns them, or you want a developer on call without hiring one full-time.
Pre-Launch Reviews & QA

A structured pre-launch review of an in-progress site — cross-browser, mobile, accessibility, performance, SEO hygiene, security basics, and content accuracy. Delivered as a prioritized punch list, not just a screenshot with an arrow.

Good fit if: You're about to launch a site built by someone else and want independent eyes before it goes live.
How consulting engagements are structured
Engagement Type A
Single
Engagement

A defined scope, a deliverable, and a clear end. Audits, reviews, one-time training sessions, and pre-launch QA all fall here. Scoped upfront, billed flat or hourly depending on what makes sense.

Common for: Tech stack audits, CMS training, vendor reviews, pre-launch QA, one-time IT ↔ Marketing facilitation
Engagement Type B
Monthly
Retainer

A fixed block of hours each month, rolled over if unused up to a limit, with priority scheduling and no minimum notice. The most flexible way to keep senior WordPress expertise on hand without the overhead of an employee.

Common for: Ongoing support, small feature work, content updates, monthly maintenance tasks, standing advisory access
Engagement Type C
Fractional
Embedded

Part-time, ongoing participation in your team's workflow — standups, sprint planning, code reviews, and direct collaboration with your internal developers or agency partners. Structured like an employee, billed like a consultant.

Common for: Teams with junior WP developers, IT departments overseeing outside agencies, orgs building an internal WordPress practice

We've talked clients out of expensive projects when simpler solutions existed. We've recommended competitors when a client's needs were genuinely a better fit elsewhere. That kind of honesty isn't noble — it's just how you build a practice that runs on referrals for fifteen years.
